Examination Rules & Regulations

Academic Policies and Examination Guidelines

Discipline Policies

Students must maintain discipline and decorum in examination halls. Any malpractice or misconduct will result in severe penalties.

Attendance and Academic Progress

Students must have at least 75% attendance to appear for university exams.

Internal Assessments

Two internal exams per semester along with assignments, attendance, and seminars contribute to the final assessment.

Examination Policy Details
Minimum Attendance 75% attendance is mandatory to appear for the exam.
Latecomers Latecomers will only be allowed if permitted by the lecturer.
Electronic Gadgets Mobile phones and electronic devices are strictly prohibited during exams.
Identity Verification Students must carry their ID cards and hall tickets at all times during examinations.
Internal Assessment Students are assessed based on two internal exams, assignments, and seminars.
Leave Policies Students must apply for leave if they are absent for more than 5 days due to illness. A medical certificate is required.
University Affiliation The College is affiliated with Mahatma Gandhi University and is an IGNOU study center.

Disciplinary Rules & Regulations

  • Students must maintain discipline both inside and outside the college.
  • Irregular attendance and disruptive behavior may lead to disciplinary action.
  • Mobile phone usage on campus is strictly prohibited.
  • Students must wear the prescribed uniform.
  • All students must participate in college events like seminars and group discussions.
  • Leave applications must be signed by the guardian or parent for approval.
  • Absence without leave from class tests, university exams, or holidays will be treated seriously.

Attendance & Academic Progress

The college works in two sessions, forenoon (3 periods) and afternoon (2 periods). The first bell is rung 5 minutes before the start of the session, and the second bell signifies the start of class. Students are expected to be seated when the first bell rings, and attendance is taken at the start of each hour. If students arrive late, they lose the attendance for that particular hour.

To qualify for university examinations, students must:

  • Have a minimum of 75% attendance in each semester as per Mahatma Gandhi University regulations.
  • Pass the college-conducted exams according to the college’s norms.
  • Have good character and conduct, as confirmed by the Principal.

Students who fail to maintain 75% attendance will not be promoted to the next semester. Absence without prior leave will be treated seriously, and any student absent from the reopening of the college after holidays will be terminated.

Four-Year Undergraduate Programme (FYUGP)

Mahatma Gandhi University (MGU), based in Kottayam, Kerala, has introduced a comprehensive FYUGP from the 2024–25 academic year. Aligned with UGC’s Curriculum and Credit Framework, this programme enhances academic flexibility, skill development, and research orientation.

Programme Structure and Pathways
  • Three-Year UG Degree: Awarded on completion of 133 credits.
  • Four-Year UG Honours: 177 credits with advanced coursework and capstone project.
  • Four-Year UG Honours with Research: 177 credits with 12-credit research project.
  • 4+1 Integrated UG-PG: Additional 40 credits for one-year PG after UG.
Curriculum Components
  • Major/Minor Courses: Primary and secondary field studies.
  • Multi-Disciplinary Courses (MDC)
  • Skill Enhancement Courses (SEC)
  • Ability Enhancement Courses (AEC)
  • Value Addition Courses (VAC)
  • Internships & Field-Based Learning
  • Research Projects/Dissertations
Assessment & Evaluation

  • Courses may use alternate evaluations over traditional exams.
Expected Outcomes
